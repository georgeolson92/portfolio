About Me

Education

Work Experience

  • During college, I worked part-time as an assistant to the student governmet communications director.
  • After graduation, I spent some time job searching and found a job working as a web designer a digital marketing agency in Vancouver, Washington. It was a very small start-up company (I started with 4 coworkers and at one point had only 2 coworkers), so wore a lot of different hats and had to teach myself a lot of new skills.
  • One skill I couldn't figure out was programming. I was able to learn the basics of HTML & CSS, but anything beyond that was difficult for me to learn on my own. So eventually, I decided I wanted to focus all of my energy into learning these new skills and teaching myself how to think & learn like a programmer.
